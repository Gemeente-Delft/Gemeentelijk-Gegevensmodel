# Introduction

Under *Contents* you will find the elaboration of the policy domains in the GGM. Only the explanations of the data definitions are included here. During the [development](../cookbook/totstandkoming.en.md) of the GGM, applications and their data were also inventoried for each policy domain. The Delft situation, including the applications used, has been worked out in each.

Bear in mind the [layered structure of the GGM](../index.en.md#structure-of-the-ggm), in which object types are decoupled across policy domains as much as possible. Only object types in the lowest layers of the model are reused by the higher layers.

![Layering of Domains][gelaagdheidDomeinen]

<em>Diagram (in Dutch): the layered structure of the GGM domains.</em>

We have decided not to include the *Applications and Policy Domains* and *Applications and Data* sections (see the [methodology](../cookbook/totstandkoming.en.md#inventorying-applications-and-policy-domains)). The *Monuments* example, however, is included [here](../monumenten.en.md).

The handbook contains diagrams of object types and their relationships. Object-type attributes are not shown — they are visible when you download and open the GGM.

Enjoy reading, and if you have comments or remarks, please file them via the [issue system](https://github.com/gemeente-delft/Gemeentelijk-Gegevensmodel/issues).
